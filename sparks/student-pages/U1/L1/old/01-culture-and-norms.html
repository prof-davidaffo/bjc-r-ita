BJC Culture and Norms

In this activity, you'll learn about the Beauty and Joy of Computing (BJC) Sparks pilot curriculum, the Snap! programming language, and the growth mindset.
  1. Watch this video from University of California, Berkeley professor and BJC curriculum designer, Dan Garcia about BJC and Snap!.
    If your connection blocks YouTube, watch the video here.
  2. Discuss the growth mindset.
    • When is a time when you used the growth mindset? How did it help you?
    • When is a time you would have benefited from thinking with a growth mindset? what could you do differently in a situation like that next time?
  3. Part of cultivating a growth mindset is creating a classroom culture that supports learning. Work with your class to develop a list of classroom norms by responding to these questions:
    • What makes it easier for you to learn?
    • What gets in the way of your learning?
    • What classroom norms could we create to facilitate your learning?
  4. All levels of computing experience are welcome in this class. To help your teacher understand your background, share your responses to these questions:
    • What is your computing experience?
    • What do you want out of this course?
In this activity, you learned that this new course is currently under development and about using the growth mindset for constructive learning and problem solving.
