Programming Your App

Welcome to BJC!  We're going to jump right in:

  1. Make sure either you or your partner is logged in to Snap! so you can save your work.
  • The stage is the white area at the top right of the Snap! window.
  • A sprite is like an actor on the stage, who can move around, and has other skills too.
  • A costume is a picture that can be worn by a sprite.

This is Alonzo, the mascot of our programming language, Snap!. You should be seeing him on the stage, the white area in the top right corner of the Snap! window. He's an animated character—you can move him around the stage by programming.

Alonzo is actually just a costume: a picture that can be worn by a sprite. The sprite is what "knows" how to move around.

  1. Program Alonzo to face the other way when clicked.
    1. The project you loaded has two sprite costumes—one facing right and the other facing left—so you can use the next costume block to make the sprite face the other way. Drag blocks into the center Scripts area to build the script shown here. (For more support, read the yellow box below.)

      This is a computer program. It's a very short one, but it'll get more interesting soon.

      You can find blocks in palettes with their matching color: purple for Looks, yellow for Control, blue for Motion, etc.

      Drag one block underneath another to snap them together. The thin white bar shows where the blocks will snap together if you release the mouse button.

      Click for a video demonstration.
    2. Un-check the "draggable" box under the sprite name input box. This will prevent players from accidentally dragging the sprite when they click it.
    3. Try it out: click Alonzo a few times and make sure he always turns to face the other way. If not, go back and figure out how to fix your code.
  2. Add the go to block to your script so that Alonzo will also move to a random position each time he's clicked.
  3. Check your program: Click on the Alonzo sprite on the white stage in the upper right area of the window. If your script works, Alonzo should face the other way and move to a random position on the stage each time you click him.
